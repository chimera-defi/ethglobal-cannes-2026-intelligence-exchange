## Intelligence Exchange Validation Plan (Go Broad -> Go Deep)

**Goal:** de-risk market, compliance, and unit economics before building heavy infrastructure.

### Phase 0: Broad Discovery (Week 1)
1. Conduct 15 interviews:
   - 8 buyers (AI-heavy startups/platform teams)
   - 5 potential sellers (infra operators/hosters)
   - 2 compliance/payment experts
2. Validate top pains:
   - routing complexity
   - reliability gaps
   - settlement complexity
3. Collect baseline metrics:
   - current monthly model spend
   - outage/failure pain
   - switching friction

### Phase 1: Problem-Solution Fit (Week 2-3)
1. Landing page + waitlist + pricing-test CTA.
2. Concierge pilot (manual routing + manual settlement) with 3-5 design partners.
3. Record hard signals:
   - willingness to route real traffic
   - willingness to pay take-rate/SLA premium
   - compliance objections

### Phase 2: Risk and Compliance Validation (Week 3-4)
1. Run policy review for supported providers and seller flows.
2. Validate no-resale/no-account-sharing controls.
3. Test dispute workflow using synthetic incidents.

### Phase 3: Unit Economics Validation (Week 4-6)
1. Measure pilot gross margin after:
   - infra
   - fraud/risk operations
   - dispute overhead
2. Define minimum viable margins by segment.
3. Finalize whether to continue, pivot, or stop.

## Interview Script (Core)
1. What workloads are most painful to route today?
2. What reliability failures caused material business impact?
3. What switching costs keep you on your current stack?
4. Would you route X% of traffic through a compliant exchange?
5. What minimum savings or SLA guarantees are required to switch?

## Success Thresholds
1. >= 5 design partners agree to pilot real traffic.
2. >= 30% of interviewed buyers indicate clear willingness to switch under target savings/SLA conditions.
3. At least one repeatable seller onboarding pattern with acceptable quality metrics.

## Kill Thresholds
1. Policy constraints prevent reliable compliant supply.
2. Buyers require guarantees that destroy margins.
3. Pilot conversion is weak despite strong stated interest.
