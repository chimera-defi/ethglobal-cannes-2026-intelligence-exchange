# Intelligence Exchange Decisions

## 2026-03-05 -- No credit resale
**Decision:** Do not model transferable provider credits/subscription resale.
**Rationale:** Provider terms commonly restrict transfer and account sharing.

## 2026-03-05 -- Exchange focus
**Decision:** Focus on compliant execution-capacity exchange + settlement.
**Rationale:** This is the viable path with defensible workflow value.

## 2026-03-05 -- Payments sequence
**Decision:** Stable settlement first, then optional utility-token coordination, agentic rails, and fiat bridge adapters.
**Rationale:** Maximizes launch clarity while preserving expansion paths without making volatile tokens the core payment unit.

## 2026-04-04 -- Stable settlement stays primary
**Decision:** Keep buyer funding, milestone reserves, and default worker payouts denominated in stable assets.
**Rationale:** Stable job pricing is required for buyer clarity, worker adoption, and defensible unit economics.

## 2026-04-04 -- Utility token is coordination, not base wages
**Decision:** Any protocol token is phase-gated and limited to staking, slashing, fee discounts, access tiers, and optional rewards. It does not replace stable settlement for core job pricing or default worker compensation.
**Rationale:** Token-denominated wages push volatility into labor pricing and do not improve real marketplace margins.

## 2026-04-04 -- Success-rights instruments stay separate
**Decision:** If the platform later offers work receipts, royalty claims, or revenue-rights products, they remain separate instruments from the utility token and outside Cannes MVP scope.
**Rationale:** Separating labor settlement from upside exposure keeps the core protocol easier to explain, audit, and operate.

## 2026-03-05 -- Validation-first
**Decision:** Require discovery and concierge pilot before heavy build.
**Rationale:** Reduces risk on compliance, liquidity, and economics.

## 2026-03-07 -- Agent-first protocol compatibility
**Decision:** Evolve from broker-only architecture to protocol-compatible broker with adapter layer.
**Rationale:** Supports user-owned black-box agents while preserving trust, settlement, and routing control.

## 2026-03-07 -- Task market modes
**Decision:** Add explicit market modes (`claim`, `bounty`, `benchmark`, `auction`) in v2.
**Rationale:** Better fit for heterogeneous agent workloads and liquidity dynamics.
